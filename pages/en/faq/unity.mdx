<Steps>

### Open Unity Project

Open Unity and load the project you want to export to WebGL.

---

### Go to Build Settings

From the top menu bar, click **File** and select **Build Settings**.

<br />
<center>![](/images/unity/unity_1.png)</center>

---

### Select WebGL Platform

In the **Build Settings** window:

- Select **WebGL** under the **Platform** section.
- If you don‚Äôt see WebGL, click **Open Download Page** to install it.
- Click **Switch Platform** to switch to WebGL.

<br />
<center>![](/images/unity/unity_2.png)</center>

---

### Adjust Player Settings (Optional)

To customize the build:

- In the **Build Settings** window, click **Player Settings**.
- Adjust settings like **Resolution and Presentation**, and **Publishing Settings** to suit your needs.

<br />
<center>![](/images/unity/unity_3.png)</center>

---

### Build the Project

- In the **Build Settings** window, click **Build**.
- Choose a directory to save the WebGL files, and Unity will start building the project in WebGL format.

---

**üìÇ Your WebGL build is ready!** You can find the files in the folder you chose, and they're ready for online upload.

---

### üîë Final Notes

<Callout type="warning">
  Ensure that **Decompression Fallback** is enabled in **Player Settings** to
  make sure your WebGL build works smoothly online.

<br />
<center>![](/images/unity/unity_4.png)</center>

</Callout>

</Steps>

## Step 2: Uploading Unity WebGL Files to Redbrick

<Steps>

### Open Redbrick Website

Open `creator.redbrick.land` in your browser.

---

### Find the Import Button

Locate the **Import** button in the left panel of the website.

<br />
<center>![](/images/unity/unity_5.png)</center>
<center>![](/images/unity/unity_6.png)</center>

---

### Select WebGL Files

Click the **Import** button to open the Import UI:

- Navigate to the folder where your WebGL files are saved.
- Select the necessary files, which will be automatically identified.

<br />
<center>![](/images/unity/unity_7.png)</center>

---

### Prepare for Project Publishing

Once all the required files are selected, proceed to the **Publish** stage:

- Enter the **name**, **description**, and **thumbnail image**.
- Click **Publish**.

<br />
<center>![](/images/unity/unity_8.png)</center>

</Steps>
